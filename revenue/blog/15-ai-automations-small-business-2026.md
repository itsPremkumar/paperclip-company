# 15 AI Automations Every Small Business Should Set Up in 2026 (Save 20+ Hours a Week)

If you run a small business, the bottleneck is almost never strategy. It's the
**same fifteen chores** eating your week: sorting inquiries, chasing invoices,
copy-pasting between apps, writing the same reply twice. In 2026 the cheapest
way to get that time back isn't hiring — it's wiring up a handful of AI and
no-code automations that run while you sleep.

This is a builder's list, not a vendor pitch. Every item below is something a
solo founder or a one-person ops team can stand up this quarter, mostly at
**zero recurring cost**, and each one points at a concrete tool. It pairs well
with our [zero-budget AI automation agency playbook](./2026-07-14-zero-budget-ai-automation-agency.md)
— that piece covers *what to sell*; this one covers *what to build first*.

## The 30-second version

- **Lead capture + triage** eats the most time and pays back fastest.
- **Content + outreach** can be 80% automated without sounding like a robot.
- **Back-office (invoicing, reconciliation, reminders)** is the highest-ROI
  because nobody enjoys it and it never stops.
- Start with **one** automation, prove the time saved, then stack the next.

## Lead generation & capture

**1. Inbound inquiry triage bot.** A form or DM auto-classifies every new lead
(cold / warm / support / wrong-fit) and drops it into the right list. Cheapest
path: a no-code tool like n8n (free, self-hosted) routing into your CRM. See our
[n8n vs Make vs Zapier breakdown](./n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md) for the
cost trade-offs.

**2. Missed-call / after-hours auto-reply.** Captures the contact, qualifies
with 2–3 questions, and books a slot — so you never lose a lead to "call us
9–5."

**3. Lead-scoring from behavior.** Tag leads who opened three emails or visited
pricing twice, and push them to the top of your follow-up queue automatically.

**4. Review-request trigger.** After a successful job, auto-send a Google/Yelp
review ask. Steady 5-star volume is the cheapest local SEO you'll ever buy.

## Sales & follow-up

**5. Abandoned-cart / quote follow-up sequence.** Three polite nudges over 7
days, then silence. Recovers revenue you already earned the right to.

**6. Proposal generator.** Turn a short intake form into a branded PDF proposal
using a templated prompt. Our [guide to packaging and selling AI prompts](./package-and-sell-ai-prompts.md)
shows the exact pattern.

**7. Objection-handler FAQ bot.** Trains on your own docs and answers the top 10
"will this work for me?" questions on the site, 24/7.

**8. Personalized cold outreach at scale.** Pull a list, generate a genuinely
relevant first line per prospect, and send in batches. Our
[cold-outreach guide](./cold-outreach-that-gets-replies.md) covers the
deliverability rules so you don't land in spam.

## Customer support

**9. Support ticket triage + canned replies.** Auto-tag urgency, suggest a
reply from your knowledge base, and only escalate the weird ones to a human.

**10. Order / booking status bot.** "Where's my stuff?" answered instantly from
your order system — kills the #1 repetitive support email.

**11. Onboarding drip.** New customer gets the right setup docs, video, and day-3
check-in without you lifting a finger.

## Operations & finance

**12. Invoice + receipt automations.** Generate, send, and reconcile invoices
against payments; flag the three that didn't clear.

**13. Expense categorization.** Bank feeds auto-sorted into categories so
month-end is a 10-minute review, not a Saturday.

**14. Social content pipeline.** One source idea becomes a post for three
platforms via a templated prompt. Our [faceless content channel guide](./faceless-ai-content-channel.md)
walks the repurposing workflow.

**15. Weekly report bot.** Rolls up revenue, pipeline, and outstanding tasks
into one Monday morning summary — no spreadsheet surgery.

## The cheapest way to build all of this

You do **not** need fifteen paid apps. The backbone is usually one
self-hostable automation tool (n8n) plus an LLM for the text bits, both of
which can run at ~$0. Layer in free tiers of your CRM and email tool and you've
got the whole stack for the price of a domain.

If you'd rather *buy* the time than build it, the same automations are exactly
what a managed [AI automation service](./2026-07-14-zero-budget-ai-automation-agency.md)
ships for clients — which is the quiet arbitrage here: learn it for yourself
free, or pay someone who already automated it.

## What to do this week

1. **Pick the one chore you hate most** from the list above.
2. **Map its steps** on paper (trigger → action → where it lives).
3. **Build the smallest version** in a free no-code tool; don't gold-plate.
4. **Measure one week** of time saved, then stack the next automation.

Most small businesses recoup 20+ hours a month from just items 1, 5, and 12.
That's half a work-week back — before you've touched the rest of the list.
