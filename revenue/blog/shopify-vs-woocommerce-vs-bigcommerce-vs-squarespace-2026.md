## 30-second verdict

- **Pick Shopify if** you want the fastest path from "I have a product" to "I have a checkout" and you'd rather pay a predictable fee than babysit servers — best for operators who want the store to just work.
- **Pick WooCommerce if** you already run WordPress, want total ownership of the data and the code, and don't mind assembling plugins — best for control freaks and content-first sites that monetize.
- **Pick BigCommerce if** you're scaling past a hobby store and want enterprise-grade native features (B2B, headless, multi-channel) without stacking paid add-ons — best for growth-stage catalogs.
- **Pick Squarespace if** your store is really a brand site with a small shop bolted on and design matters more than deep commerce config — best for creators and portfolio-commerce.

None of these is "the thing that prints money" on its own — a storefront is the conversion layer, and it only pays off when a [lead-generation system](./build-ai-lead-generation-system-2026.md) feeds it traffic and a [content repurposing engine](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) keeps that traffic flowing. It sits one layer *above* the [newsletter platforms](./beehiiv-vs-substack-vs-convertkit-vs-ghost-2026.md) and [email tools](./mailchimp-vs-klaviyo-vs-activecampaign-vs-brevo-2026.md) that bring buyers back.

## How to choose without overthinking

Start from the job, not the feature list:

1. **"I want a store live this weekend with zero server talk."** Shopify — the hosted checkout and app store mean you're taking orders before lunch, not after a plugin conflict.
2. **"I already have a WordPress site and want to own everything."** WooCommerce — your product data lives in your own database and you can automate it with the same [n8n vs Make vs Zapier](./n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md) wiring you use everywhere else.
3. **"I'm past 500 SKUs and need B2B, headless, and multi-channel without an add-on tax."** BigCommerce — the native feature depth means fewer paid apps bleeding margin.
4. **"My shop is 10 products and the brand is the point."** Squarespace — the design quality and all-in-one billing beat wrestling a commerce plugin for a side storefront.

If you're running lean, the right call is the cheapest plan that lets you list a product and connect a payment method. The platform is a funnel layer, not the product — spend your energy on the [faceless channel](./faceless-youtube-vs-tiktok-vs-newsletter-2026.md) that drives traffic and the [customer records](./hubspot-vs-salesforce-vs-pipedrive-vs-zoho-2026.md) that turn one-time buyers into repeat revenue.
