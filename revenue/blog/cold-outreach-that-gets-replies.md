# Cold Outreach That Actually Gets Replies (Templates + the System Behind Them)

*Category: AI Business / Lead-Gen · Part of the "Prem Autonomous Co" content funnel.*

Cold outreach has a reputation problem: spammy, low-reply, soul-draining. The
reason most outreach fails isn't the channel — it's that it's written by someone
who hasn't done the research and can't be bothered to personalize. The fix
isn't a "hack." It's a **repeatable system** that researches first, writes
second, and sends at a human volume. Here's the exact playbook our agents run
to produce reply-worthy sequences — and the ready-to-send pack that ships with it.

## Why most cold outreach fails

- **Spray-and-pray.** High volume, zero relevance. Inboxes filter it; humans
  ignore it. Reply rates collapse below 1%.
- **Generic first line.** "I hope this finds you well" is a tax on the reader's
  patience. It signals you didn't look.
- **No clear next step.** If the reader doesn't know what to do in 5 seconds,
  they don't do it.
- **No follow-up.** Most replies come from the 2nd or 3rd touch. Stop at one and
  you've done 70% of the work for 30% of the result.

The system below attacks all four at once — and it's built so an agent can
generate it from a brief, not from a blank page.

## The 4-step outreach system (zero cost)

### 1. Research before you write
Never open the compose box without a one-line note on *why this person*. Pull a
specific detail: a recent launch, a job post, a post they wrote. That detail is
your opener. No detail → skip them. Targeting beats volume every time.

### 2. Lead with the cost of inaction
Open on the pain, not on you. "Most teams your size lose 10–15 hrs/week to
[task]" lands harder than "We help companies." Then offer the smallest possible
next step: a 15-min call or a free teardown, never a demo-of-everything.

### 3. Sequence, don't single-shot
Plan three touches across 10 days:

| Touch | Day | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | Open with specificity + one-line offer |
| 2 | 4 | Nudge with the ROI/proof angle |
| 3 | 9 | Break-up note ("should I close the loop?") |

The break-up email is the highest-reply touch in the sequence. Don't skip it.

### 4. Keep volume human
Send 10–25/day per identity, not 500. Replies scale with relevance, not with
spam filters. An agent can draft and personalize; a human owns the mailbox and
the send. **Money and sending stay human** — the agent only prepares.

## The honest part

Outreach is the *top* of the funnel — it earns the conversation, it doesn't
close it. It works best when it points at a real asset (a calculator, a
teardown, a product) instead of "book a call" vapor. Automate the *drafting*;
stay human on the *sending* and the *money*.
