## 30-second verdict

- **Pick Framer if** you want a design-led, browser-native site that looks like a custom build and you'll let AI draft the layout — best for landing pages and portfolios where visual polish sells.
- **Pick Webflow if** you need a real CMS, cleaner code export, and team-grade control — best for content-heavy marketing sites that have to scale past a one-pager.
- **Pick Wix (AI) if** you're non-technical and want a finished site in minutes with the least learning curve — best for local businesses and quick experiments, at the cost of flexibility.
- **Pick 10Web if** you already live in WordPress but want AI to rebuild and host it — best for anyone who needs WP plugins and themes without hand-coding.

None of these is "the AI that runs your business" — they're the storefront layer. Pair them with the automation underneath (see our [autonomous AI business stack](./autonomous-ai-business-stack-2026.md)) so the site actually captures and routes leads instead of just sitting there.

## How to choose without overthinking

Start from the job, not the feature list:

1. **"I need a beautiful one-pager yesterday."** Framer or Wix AI. Framer if design matters, Wix if you want zero decisions.
2. **"I'll publish 50 blog posts and need structure."** Webflow — its visual CMS is the differentiator and it won't fight you at scale.
3. **"I already run plugins, bookings, and a store on WordPress."** 10Web — you keep the ecosystem and just get AI to do the heavy lifting.

If you're running a [zero-budget AI company](./how-to-run-ai-company-zero-budget.md), the right call is usually the cheapest plan that lets you connect a custom domain and a form. The site is a funnel entry point, not the product. Spend your energy on the [lead-generation system](./build-ai-lead-generation-system-2026.md) and [content repurposing](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) that feed it.
