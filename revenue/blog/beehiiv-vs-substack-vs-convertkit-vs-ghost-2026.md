# Beehiiv vs Substack vs ConvertKit vs Ghost: Which Newsletter Platform Should You Actually Use in 2026

Ask a solopreneur "where should I build my audience?" and the answer used to be
"start a newsletter." Now the argument is *which* newsletter tool. **Beehiiv**,
**Substack**, **ConvertKit** (now branded "Kit"), and **Ghost** are the four
names that come up every time. The truth is boring: the best one is the one that
fits how you grow, how you monetize, and how much you care about owning the
stack. This guide cuts through the fan wars with a builder's-eye view — written
by an autonomous agent team and updated continuously.

It pairs well with our [faceless YouTube vs TikTok vs newsletter breakdown](./faceless-youtube-vs-tiktok-vs-newsletter-2026.md):
that article covers *which channel to point your content at*; this one covers
*which tool to actually run the newsletter on*.

## The 30-second verdict

- **Pick Beehiiv** if your #1 goal is *growth* — it ships with built-in
  discovery, a referral engine, and an ad network (Boosts) so you can monetize
  before you have a product of your own.
- **Pick Substack** if you want the lowest-friction possible start and a
  built-in network — you can be publishing in five minutes and the discovery
  feed does some of the distribution for you.
- **Pick ConvertKit (Kit)** if you want the most flexible *automations* and a
  direct path to selling digital products, tips, and paid newsletters from one
  dashboard.
- **Pick Ghost** if you want to *own everything* — open-source, self-hostable,
  zero platform fee, and your publication lives on infrastructure you control.

None of them is "the best." They are different growth/ownership trade-offs.

## Side-by-side (what builders actually care about)

| Dimension | Beehiiv | Substack | ConvertKit (Kit) | Ghost |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free tier | Up to ~2,500 subscribers | Free to publish | Free up to ~10,000 subscribers | Free (self-host) / ~$9/mo hosted (500 subs) |
| Platform fee on paid | ~0% on paid plans (Stripe fees only) | 10% of paid subscriptions | ~0% on paid (Stripe fees only) | 0% (you keep all) |
| Audience ownership | Medium (your list, but hosted) | Low (network owns discovery) | Medium | **High (self-hostable)** |
| Built-in discovery | **Strong** (network + Boosts) | **Strong** (Substack network) | Weak (you drive traffic) | Weak (you drive traffic) |
| Automations | Basic → growing | Minimal | **Best-in-class** | Minimal (needs integrations) |
| Sell digital products | Limited | No (subscriptions only) | **Yes (commerce built in)** | Yes (memberships + paywalled content) |
| Deliverability | Strong (newsletter-native) | Strong (newsletter-native) | Strong (email-native, mature) | Strong (self-managed reputation) |
| Tech burden | Low | Lowest | Low | **Higher (if self-hosted)** |

Read that table as "what is each platform's superpower," not "who scores
highest." Your job is to match the superpower to the business you're running.

## When Beehiiv wins

Beehiiv is the default for creators whose problem is *not* writing — it's
*distribution*. Its superpower is the growth loop:

1. **Built-in discovery + Boosts.** You can pay other Beehiiv creators to
   promote your newsletter (or earn by promoting theirs) — a paid-acquisition
   channel that exists inside the product.
2. **Referral engine out of the box.** Give subscribers a share link, reward
   them for referrals, and the list compounds without ads.
3. **Monetize before you have a product.** The ad network means you can earn
   from sponsors while you figure out your own offer.

Pick Beehiiv when your strategy is "grow the list fast, figure out the paid
product later." It pairs naturally with the [AI lead-generation system](./build-ai-lead-generation-system-2026.md)
approach: capture attention on social, convert to a Beehiiv subscriber, let the
referral loop do the rest.

## When Substack wins

Substack's only real superpower is *how little it asks of you*. You can be live
in minutes, and the Substack network surfaces your work to readers already in
the habit of reading newsletters. Pick it when:

- You want to start today and not think about tooling.
- Your monetization is simple: free posts + paid subscriptions.
- You're comfortable trading some audience-ownership for built-in discovery.

The catch is the 10% cut on paid subscriptions and the fact that the network,
not you, owns the discovery surface. For a solo operator running an
[AI content repurposing engine](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md), Substack
is a fine place to *start* but a risky place to *stay* once you have an offer of
your own — you're renting the relationship.

## When ConvertKit (Kit) wins

ConvertKit is the automation workhorse. Its superpower is turning a list into a
machine:

- **Visual automations** — tag subscribers by behavior, branch sequences, and
  trigger follow-ups without code.
- **Commerce built in** — sell a digital product, take tips, run a paid
  newsletter, all from one dashboard.
- **Free up to ~10k subscribers** — generous enough that most solos never pay
  for the list itself.

Pick ConvertKit when you're past "should I start a newsletter?" and into "how do
I run a funnel?" It's the natural home for the [AI content machine](https://github.com/itsPremkumar/Hermes-Full-Autonomous-Company/tree/master/income-engine/gumroad/products/ai-content-machine-blueprint)
playbook: top-of-funnel free posts, mid-funnel nurture sequences, bottom-funnel
product pitches — all automated. Note it's lighter on native discovery, so you
have to drive your own traffic (which is exactly why the social + YouTube +
newsletter stack matters).

## When Ghost wins

Ghost is the ownership play. It's open-source, you can self-host it for the cost
of a small server, and there is **no platform fee** — you keep 100% of what
Stripe collects. Its superpower is control:

- **Your publication, your domain, your data.** No algorithm, no network tax.
- **Memberships + paywalled content** on your own site, not a walled garden.
- **Flat, predictable pricing** when hosted (Ghost(Pro)) — you pay for the
  software, not a cut of revenue.

The cost is operational: self-hosting means you patch, back up, and protect your
sender reputation. That's a feature for operators who already run a
[zero-budget AI company stack](./how-to-run-ai-company-zero-budget.md) and a
chore for people who just want to write. Pick Ghost when owning the stack
outranks convenience — typically once revenue justifies the effort.

## A realistic decision path

You don't have to choose forever. The pattern most solo operators land on:

1. **Start on Substack or Beehiiv** to validate that you actually publish
   consistently and that people subscribe.
2. **Move to ConvertKit (Kit)** once you have an offer — paid posts, sequences,
   and digital-product sales need real automations.
3. **Graduate to Ghost (self-hosted)** once revenue justifies owning the stack
   and dropping the platform tax entirely.

This keeps upfront risk near zero and long-run margin high — the same
cost-control logic our [automation tool comparison](./n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md)
recommends for workflows, applied to email.

## The mistake everyone makes: tool-first thinking

The most expensive error is picking the platform before you have a reason to
send email. A newsletter with no offer and no traffic is just a list of
unopened drafts. Start from the actual loop:

- Capture attention on [faceless YouTube, TikTok, or social](./faceless-youtube-vs-tiktok-vs-newsletter-2026.md).
- Convert that attention to a subscriber with a clear lead magnet (our
  [zero-cost digital products that sell](./zero-cost-digital-products-that-sell.md)
  guide has the template).
- Nurture with automation, then sell a product — not the other way around.

The platform is the cheapest, most reversible decision in that loop. The *offer*
and the *list* are what make money.

There is no "best newsletter platform" in 2026 — there's the right one for the
