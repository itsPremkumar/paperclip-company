# Otter.ai vs Fireflies.ai vs Fathom vs tl;dv (2026): Which AI Meeting Notetaker Actually Earns Its Seat?

If you're running [seven workflows solopreneurs automate first](./7-workflows-solopreneurs-automate-first.md) or building a [zero-budget lead-generation system](./build-ai-lead-generation-system-2026.md), the meeting is where the money is — and where the notes go to die. ## The 30-second version

- **Otter.ai:** the live-transcription king — real-time captions, the strongest standalone search/note experience, and the safest default when you just need every word written down; but its CRM and team features are lighter than the dedicated hubs.
- **Fireflies.ai:** the CRM-logging hub — it sits on every call, auto-summarizes, and pushes the action items into your stack (Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack); the choice when the *output* needs to land in a pipeline, not just a doc.
- **Fathom:** the free, Zoom-native recorder — one-click, no-seat-cost capture with solid summaries and CRM sync on paid tiers; the obvious first pick for a solo rep who wants recordings without a bill.
- **tl;dv:** the async video-review specialist — timestamped highlights and shareable clip reels so a busy manager watches the 90 seconds that matter; the pick when the *audience* is people who won't read a transcript.

None is "the best." They solve different jobs. The rest tells you exactly which to reach for.

## Match the notetaker to the job

### 1. You just need every word, fast, on any call → **Otter.ai**
When the priority is *capture and find*, Otter's live captions and search beat everyone. If you're a founder doing [cold outreach that gets replies](./cold-outreach-that-gets-replies.md) and then hopping on discovery calls, Otter makes sure the promise you made at minute twelve is searchable in month three. Use it as the raw record; then let the [content repurposing engine](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) turn the best moments into posts.

### 2. The notes must land in your pipeline, not a doc → **Fireflies.ai**
If you run a sales or support motion where the *action item* is the product, Fireflies is the hub: it joins the call, writes the summary, and pushes the follow-up into HubSpot, Salesforce, or Slack so nothing relies on a human remembering. This is the transcript half of [packaging and selling AI prompts](./package-and-sell-ai-prompts.md) — pair the recap with the [n8n automation](./n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md) that fires the follow-up. Same logic as the [customer-support blueprint](./ai-customer-support-zero-budget-2026.md): capture, route, close.

### 3. You're a solo rep who refuses to pay for recordings → **Fathom**
Fathom's free tier is the lowest-friction way to get a clean recording plus a usable summary on Zoom, and it upgrades to CRM sync when you're ready. It's the right first tool for the [solopreneur who automates first](./7-workflows-solopreneurs-automate-first.md) and doesn't want another subscription before the revenue shows up. Start free, graduate later — the opposite of the lock-in trap we warn about in the [autonomous business stack](./autonomous-ai-business-stack-2026.md).

### 4. Your audience won't read a transcript → **tl;dv**
Managers, clients, and coaches don't open 4,000-word transcripts. tl;dv's timestamped highlights and shareable clip reels let you send "watch the 90 seconds where we agreed on pricing" instead of "here's the doc." That's the distribution half of [faceless content](./faceless-ai-content-channel.md): the call *is* the content, and the reel is the edit. Feed the best clips into the [content repurposing engine](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) and you've got a weeks-long posting queue from one conversation.

## A realistic cost picture (2026)

- **Otter.ai:** credit/usage-based — generous for prototyping, but transcription minutes add up the moment you record daily calls or long workshops. Predictable enough, but you pay whether the recap ships or not.
- **Fireflies.ai:** per-seat pricing — the value scales with the *team and the pipeline it feeds*, not with raw minutes, which makes it cheaper than it looks once you count the manual CRM entry it removes.
- **Fathom:** free-first with paid CRM/sync tiers — the cheapest way to start, and the upgrade is a tax on *growth*, not on *experimentation*.
- **tl;dv:** free tier plus paid for advanced clipping/hub features — you pay for the *editing and sharing*, not the recording.

Rule of thumb: if the recap is *the artifact* (a summary you reread, search, and quote), Otter earns its minutes on the first call you actually reference. The moment the recap is *a handoff to a pipeline or a person who won't read*, Fireflies or tl;dv pays for itself by moving the output instead of storing it. Same economic logic we lay out for [self-hosted stacks](./autonomous-ai-business-stack-2026.md): pick the tool whose bill scales with *output that sells*, not with *experimentation*.

## The decision shortcut

> **Pick Otter.ai** if you need the best live transcription and search for any call.
> **Pick Fireflies.ai** if the notes must auto-land in your CRM, Slack, or ticketing stack.
> **Pick Fathom** if you want free, one-click Zoom capture before you've earned revenue.
> **Pick tl;dv** if your audience consumes highlights as video, not text.

Most solo operators we know run a **Fathom-for-capture + Fireflies-for-handoff** split once they have a team, or stay on **Fathom-free until the first deal closes**. You're not loyal to a notetaker — you're loyal to the follow-up and the margin.
