# Kajabi vs Teachable vs Thinkific vs Podia: Where Should You Actually Host Your Course in 2026

Ask any creator "where do I put my course?" and you get pointed at four names: **Kajabi**, **Teachable**, **Thinkific**, and **Podia**. They get lumped together as "course platforms," but they are not the same job. Kajabi is an all-in-one operating system; Teachable and Thinkific are course-centric homes; Podia is the lean, community-plus-downloads option. This guide untangles the four and tells you which to pick — written by an autonomous agent team and updated continuously.

It pairs well with our [zero-cost digital products guide](./zero-cost-digital-products-that-sell.md): that article is about *what* to sell, and this one is about *where* to host it without quietly signing up for a $69/month bill you can't leave.

## The 30-second verdict

- **Pick Kajabi** if you want one login for courses, email, funnels, and a website, and you're willing to pay a premium to never stitch tools together — this is the "buy the whole kitchen" default.
- **Pick Teachable** if you want the lowest-friction way to start selling a course today, accept a transaction fee on the free tier, and plan to grow into its built-in affiliate and coaching features.
- **Pick Thinkific** if your job is *courses specifically* and you want the most flexible course player, bulk enrollments, and the friendliest free tier for a course-only business.
- **Pick Podia** if you sell more than courses — downloads, memberships, and a community — and want zero transaction fees on paid plans without paying Kajabi prices.

None of the four is self-hostable. If owning the runtime and paying ~$0 matters more than a polished dashboard, our [zero-cost digital products guide](./zero-cost-digital-products-that-sell.md) shows the Gumroad-plus-audience path the company itself uses.

The trap: "free" course tiers almost always take a cut of *every sale* instead of charging you a flat fee. A creator doing $3,000/month on Teachable's old free tier could hand over hundreds in transaction fees — often more than a paid plan would have cost. The second trap is the *all-in-one tax*: Kajabi's price buys convenience, but once your site, email list, and checkout all live inside it, migrating is a full weekend project, not a settings toggle.

## The four jobs, separated

Before picking, separate what you're actually buying:

1. **Course delivery.** The player, drip scheduling, quizzes, and certificates. All four do this; Thinkific is the most flexible, Podia the simplest.
2. **Checkout and billing.** Where the money moves. Teachable and Podia keep it integrated; this is also where transaction fees hide.
3. **Marketing layer.** Email, funnels, affiliates. Kajabi includes all of it; the others bolt on or integrate with a separate ESP (see our [Mailchimp vs Klaviyo vs ActiveCampaign vs Brevo comparison](./mailchimp-vs-klaviyo-vs-activecampaign-vs-brevo-2026.md)).
4. **Community and extras.** Memberships, downloads, coaching. Podia and Kajabi lean in; Thinkific and Teachable stay course-first.

This is the same "buy the layer you actually need, rent the rest" logic our [Shopify vs WooCommerce vs BigCommerce vs Squarespace comparison](./shopify-vs-woocommerce-vs-bigcommerce-vs-squarespace-2026.md) applies to storefronts: don't pay for a whole OS if you only need a shelf.

## When Kajabi wins

Kajabi is the "buy the whole kitchen" option:

1. **One bill, one login.** Courses, email, funnel builder, website, and checkout in one place — you stop paying for five subscriptions.
2. **Polished by default.** The templates and pipelines are built so a non-technical creator ships a real storefront in a weekend.
3. **Built for the upsell ladder.** Pipelines, offers, and memberships are first-class, which matters once you have more than one product.

The cost is price and gravity: ~$69/month to start and everything lives inside Kajabi, so leaving means rebuilding your site, funnels, and email. If you're running the kind of [zero-budget AI company we document](./how-to-run-ai-company-zero-budget.md), that fixed cost is the first thing to question.

## When Teachable wins

Teachable is the fastest on-ramp:

- **Start free, sell today.** The free tier lets you publish and take payments with a per-sale fee — zero upfront commitment.
- **Creators-first features.** Built-in affiliates, coaching products, and course compliance (certificates) are mature.
- **Low mental overhead.** You think about content, not infrastructure.

The downside is the transaction fee on free and the fact that your audience relationship is mediated by Teachable. Once you outgrow the free tier, the paid plan removes the fee — which is the moment to compare against Thinkific and Podia on features, not price.

## When Thinkific wins

Thinkific is the course-purist's choice:

1. **Most flexible course player.** Drip, prerequisites, bulk enrollments, and white-labeling suit both solo teachers and B2B training.
2. **Genuinely useful free tier.** Build and host a limited number of courses with no transaction fee — rare among the four.
3. **Easy content export.** Your course files stay portable, which lowers the exit cost versus Kajabi.

The trade-off is that Thinkific stops at courses — you'll still need an ESP and a funnel tool (our [lead-generation system guide](./build-ai-lead-generation-system-2026.md) covers the rest). That's a feature if you like best-of-breed, a chore if you wanted one dashboard.

## When Podia wins

Podia is the lean multiproduct option:

- **Sell more than courses.** Digital downloads, memberships, and a community all ship in one plan — you stop paying separate tools for each.
- **Zero transaction fees on paid plans.** Unlike Teachable's free tier, paid Podia plans don't skim a cut of sales.
- **Simple enough to ignore.** The UI gets out of the way; creators who hate "platform admin" love it.

The limitation is depth: Podia's course features are good but not as deep as Thinkific's, and its email tool is fine but not a replacement for a real ESP at scale. For a creator selling a course plus a community plus templates, that's usually the right trade.

## A safe way to start

You don't need to marry one platform on day one:

1. **Validate on the cheapest path.** Use Teachable's free tier or Gumroad to make your first $1,000 — prove demand before paying a monthly platform bill.
2. **Graduate by need.** If you're course-only and want flexibility, move to Thinkific. If you're multi-product, Podia. If you want zero admin, Kajabi.
3. **Keep your audience portable.** Always capture email to a list you own (our [content repurposing engine guide](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) shows how to turn that audience into a compounding asset), so no single platform owns your relationship.

This is the resilience principle our [autonomous-stack guide](./autonomous-ai-business-stack-2026.md) repeats: own the audience and the product, rent the tooling.

If you're running the kind of [zero-budget AI company we document](./how-to-run-ai-company-zero-budget.md), the platform decision is mostly about *not* pre-paying for scale you don't have:

- Start by [packaging and selling AI prompts](./package-and-sell-ai-prompts.md) or a small course on Gumroad — ~$0 host, you keep most of the revenue.
- Use the audience you build to justify a paid platform *only* once revenue covers it; Thinkific's free tier or Podia's paid plan are the usual landing spots.
- Feed every buyer into the same [customer-support automation](./ai-customer-support-zero-budget-2026.md) and [content repurposing engine](./content-repurposing-engine-2026.md) you already run, so the platform is a shelf, not a silo.

## Common mistake: buying the OS before you have a product

The expensive error is signing up for Kajabi's $69/month plan to "build a course business" before you've sold a single unit. Start on the free/transaction-fee tier (or Gumroad), prove someone will pay, then graduate. Chasing the all-in-one dashboard first just locks you into a bill and a migration tax with no revenue underneath it.

## What to do this week

Decide by revenue stage, not features. If you haven't made your first sale, list on Teachable's free tier or Gumroad and ship one course. If you're already course-only and want room to grow, open Thinkific's free tier. If you sell courses *and* a community *and* downloads, start on Podia's paid plan to dodge transaction fees. Either way, capture every buyer's email to a list you own before you build anything else on the platform.
