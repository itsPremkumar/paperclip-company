# 7 Workflows Solopreneurs Should Automate First With AI Agents (Zero Budget)

*Category: AI Business / Automation · Part of the "Prem Autonomous Co" content funnel.*

Most "automate your business" advice starts with the wrong question: *what's the flashiest bot I can build?* The right question is cheaper and more boring — *which repetitive job is quietly eating the hours I should be spending on revenue?*

You don't need a $500/mo stack or a dev team. Every workflow below can be wired up for $0 using agents and free-tier tools, and most of them we run ourselves. Here are the seven we'd automate first if we were starting a one-person business today.

## Why these seven (and not the other 50)

A workflow is worth automating on day one only if it is (1) repetitive, (2) rules-based enough that a mistake is cheap to catch, and (3) directly tied to either *making money* or *freeing time to make money*. Vanity automations (auto-posting inspirational quotes) don't make the cut. These do.

## The seven

### 1. Lead capture → CRM → follow-up
The classic. A form submission lands in a spreadsheet or free CRM, an agent enriches it (finds the LinkedIn, tags the niche), and a sequenced follow-up goes out. This is the single highest-ROI automation for any service business.
*Tooling:* free form tool + [n8n/Make/Zapier](n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md) + a draft sequence from our [cold outreach pack](package-and-sell-ai-prompts.md).

### 2. Cold outreach that actually gets replies
Founders lose weeks hand-writing the same email. An agent can personalize at scale using only public info, then route replies to you. The skill is in the *constraints*, not the volume — keep it human, keep it short.
*Start here:* [Cold outreach that gets replies](cold-outreach-that-gets-replies.md).

### 3. Content repurposing
Write once, ship everywhere. One article becomes a thread, a Short script, an email, and a carousel — all drafted by an agent and queued for your approval. This is how a solo operator keeps a presence on five channels.
*See the engine:* [Faceless AI content channel](faceless-ai-content-channel.md).

### 4. Invoice & expense triage
Not "let the agent pay your bills" (never — that's a human gate). But sorting, categorize, flagging duplicates, and drafting the monthly snapshot is pure rules work an agent eats for breakfast.

### 5. Customer FAQ & first-line support
An agent answers the 80% of questions that are the same every time, and escalates the weird 20% to you with full context. Response time drops from "someday" to "instant."

### 6. Product packaging from existing content
You already have the value — it's scattered in docs, threads, and notes. An agent assembles it into a sellable pack (Markdown + CSV + README + license) in minutes. We've shipped eight this way.
*The playbook:* [Zero-cost digital products that sell](zero-cost-digital-products-that-sell.md).

### 7. The weekly review loop
Every Friday an agent summarizes what happened — pipeline, published content, open tasks — into one page you actually read. Most solopreneurs skip the review and repeat the same mistakes. Automate the remembering.

## How we run it (the zero-cost part)

None of this needs a proprietary platform. The pattern is: a free orchestration tool (the [n8n vs Make vs Zapier](n8n-vs-make-vs-zapier-2026.md) breakdown helps you pick) + an agent for the judgment calls + a human approving anything that moves money or sends to a real person. That last part is non-negotiable — the autonomy loop we run keeps a human on every cash and send gate.

## The honest part

Automation won't save a business with no offer. Do these *after* you've manually done the workflow ten times and proven it makes money. Automate the proven, not the hypothetical. And watch the agent's work for the first two weeks — cheap mistakes are only cheap if you catch them early.
