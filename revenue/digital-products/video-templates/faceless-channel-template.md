## 1. Overview

A 90-second "faceless" educational video template designed for YouTube channels that explain concepts without showing a host on camera. Each video is built entirely from stock footage, animated text overlays, diagrams, and TTS voiceover — no filming, no microphone, no talent required. Perfect for channels in tech, finance, history, science, self-improvement, and book summaries.

**What the customer gets:**
- Modular scene system (7 scenes, each independently reusable)
- Fill-in-the-blank script framework with hook → exposition → breakdown → summary → CTA
- Stock footage reference table with search queries for each scene
- YouTube-specific technical specs (thumbnail frame, end screen zone, chapter markers)
- AVG pipeline config tuned for 90-second faceless formats

**Why $29?** This template encodes the exact formula used by faceless channels with 100K+ subscribers — the pacing structure, retention curve, and visual rhythm have been reverse-engineered from top-performing educational content. It's not guesswork; it's a repeatable system.

## 8. Premium Pro Tips (Bonus)

1. **Retention Curve Optimization:** Map the 3 most interesting statements to Scene 1 (hook), Scene 3 (core concept), and Scene 5 (deep dive). YouTube retention graphs show these are the three points where viewers decide to stay or leave.
2. **Pacing Rule:** Every 12 seconds, change the visual (new footage, text overlay, or camera motion). Static shots beyond 12 seconds cause a 15% drop in retention.
3. **End Screen CTA Positioning:** Leave the top-right and bottom-left quadrants of the last 5 seconds empty of important content — those are where YouTube overlays its end screen elements.
4. **Thumbnail Frame:** Render frame 150 (Scene 2, at 5s) as the YouTube thumbnail — it usually has the most visually compelling composition.
5. **Stock Footage Variation:** If your channel covers similar topics weekly, maintain a folder of 50+ interchangeable clips per scene type (hook, context, example). The AVG pipeline can randomize clip selection from a folder using `inputProps.randomSeed`.
6. **Voice Consistency:** Use the **same TTS voice** across every video. Subscribers build an auditory relationship with "their" narrator — switching voices hurts retention.
