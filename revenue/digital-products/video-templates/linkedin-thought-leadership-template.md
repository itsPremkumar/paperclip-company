## 1. Overview

A 75-second thought leadership video template designed for professionals building their personal brand on LinkedIn. Instead of a "talking head" video, this template uses animated text overlays, data visualizations, stock footage metaphors, and TTS voiceover — the creator never needs to appear on camera. The content structure follows LinkedIn's proven viral formula: **Contrarian Hook → Credibility Setup → Core Insight → Actionable Framework → Engagement CTA**.

**What the customer gets:**
- 3 aspect ratio variants (16:9 for LinkedIn video tab, 1:1 for feed, 4:5 for mobile-feed dominance)
- Fill-in-the-blank script structured around LinkedIn's viral content patterns
- Visual direction guide with "professional" stock metaphor references
- LinkedIn-specific posting strategy (caption framework, hashtag set, best posting times)
- AVG pipeline config with LinkedIn subtitle and branding specs

**Why $29?** Thought leadership is the #1 performing content type on LinkedIn in 2025, but most professionals don't have a video production setup. This template lets anyone publish a production-quality thought leadership video every week using only stock footage and their ideas — no camera, no studio, no video editing skills.

## 3. Script Template (Fill-in-the-Blank)

> **Instructions for the customer:** Replace `[...]` placeholders. The script uses a "teaching from experience" tone — not "I'm an expert" but "here's what I've observed."  
> **TTS voice recommendation:** `en-US-GuyNeural` (authoritative male — best for LinkedIn professional content) or `en-US-AriaNeural` (warm female, confident).

### SCENE 1 — Contrarian Hook (0:00–0:10) | Words: ~25–30

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — BOLD, ANIMATED IN]**
> **"The biggest myth about [Topic]: [Myth]"**

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "The biggest myth about `[Topic]`? That `[Myth]`. But after `[X years / experience]`, I've learned the opposite is true."

**Script Variables:**
- `[Topic]` — your subject area (e.g., "leadership," "sales," "career growth")
- `[Myth]` — conventional wisdom you disagree with
- `[X years / experience]` — your credibility qualifier (e.g., "10 years in SaaS sales," "advising 50+ founders")

---

### SCENE 2 — Credibility Setup (0:10–0:22) | Words: ~35–40

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "Here's why I say this. Over `[time period]`, I've worked with `[number]` `[clients/companies/professionals]` and watched them `[common mistake]`. Every single time, the `[intuitive approach]` failed. And the `[counterintuitive approach]` won."

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — DATA/STAT CALL-OUT]**
> **"[Number]% / [X] out of [Y]"** — big stat, accent color

**Scene Variables:**
- `[time period]` — e.g., "the last 5 years," "my career at McKinsey"
- `[number]` + `[clients/companies]` — credibility data
- `[common mistake]` — what people do wrong
- `[intuitive approach]` / `[counterintuitive approach]` — the contrast

---

### SCENE 3 — The Core Insight (0:22–0:40) | Words: ~40–50

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "The core insight is simple: `[core argument]`. Most people focus on `[common focus]`, but what actually matters is `[real driver]`. Let me explain why."

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — TWO-COLUMN COMPARISON]**
> **"Most people do: [Wrong approach]"**  
> **"High performers do: [Right approach]"**

**Scene Variables:**
- `[core argument]` — your main insight in 1 sentence
- `[common focus]` — what the industry gets wrong
- `[real driver]` — the actual success factor
- `[Wrong approach]` / `[Right approach]` — contrasting behaviors

---

### SCENE 4 — Actionable Framework (0:40–1:00) | Words: ~35–45

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "Here's a simple framework I use: `[Framework Name]`. Step one — `[Step 1]`. Step two — `[Step 2]`. Step three — `[Step 3]`. That's it. When you follow this, `[transformed outcome]`."

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — 3-STEP FRAMEWORK, ANIMATED]**
> **Step 1: [Action]**  
> **Step 2: [Action]**  
> **Step 3: [Action]**

**Scene Variables:**
- `[Framework Name]` — e.g., "The 3R Framework," "The ICE Method"
- `[Step 1/2/3]` — three action steps, each 2–5 words
- `[transformed outcome]` — what happens when they do it

---

### SCENE 5 — Personal Proof / Case Study (1:00–1:12) | Words: ~25–30

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "I saw this play out with `[client / company / person]`. They were struggling with `[challenge]`. But once they implemented `[framework name]`, they `[specific result]` in just `[timeframe]`."

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — TESTIMONIAL / RESULT CALL-OUT]**
> **"Result: [Metric]"** — big number, e.g., "3x revenue in 6 months"

**Scene Variables:**
- `[client / company / person]` — anonymized case study
- `[challenge]` — their starting situation
- `[framework name]` — reference to Scene 4
- `[specific result]` + `[timeframe]` — quantified outcome

---

### SCENE 6 — Engagement CTA (1:12–1:15) | Words: ~10–15

> **[VOICEOVER]**
> "What's your take? Have you seen this in your industry? Drop a comment below — I read every single one."

> **[TEXT OVERLAY — LINKEDIN-FRIENDLY CTA]**
> **"What do you think? ↓ Comment below"**

**Scene Variables:**
- None required — the CTA is standardized for LinkedIn engagement. Customer can customize the question.

## 9. Premium Pro Tips (Bonus)

1. **The Comment Hook:** The CTA question is the single most important line. Questions that start with "What's one thing..." or "Have you noticed..." get 3x more comments than yes/no questions.
2. **First 3 Seconds Rule:** LinkedIn autoplays silently in-feed. The hook text MUST be readable within 2.5 seconds or viewers scroll past. No fade-in delays.
3. **Caption Carries the Video:** Post the script as the caption — LinkedIn's algorithm ranks posts higher when the video and caption share semantic keywords.
4. **4:5 Aspect Ratio for Mobile:** 4:5 (1080×1350) fills more of the LinkedIn mobile feed than 1:1. This is the highest-CTR aspect ratio for LinkedIn native video in 2025.
5. **Document-to-Video Repurposing:** Turn existing LinkedIn carousels and PDF documents into video scripts using the AVG pipeline's document parser. Paste your long-form post, and it auto-extracts the hook → insight → framework.
6. **External SRT File:** Don't burn in subtitles for LinkedIn — provide a separate .SRT file. LinkedIn's native closed captions are preferred by the algorithm and accessible to viewers who use text-to-speech.
